Republic Act No. 9208

This expert system will cover the Republic act No. 9208, also known as the "Anti-Trafficking in Persons Act of 2003," was enacted on May 26, 2003, to address the growing issue of human trafficking in the Philippines, particularly the trafficking of women and children. This law aims to eliminate trafficking by establishing policies and institutional mechanisms to protect and support victims. It sets out penalties for individuals involved in trafficking and outlines the responsibilities of the government and other entities in preventing trafficking, ensuring the recovery and reintegration of victims, and prosecuting offenders. RA 9208 represents a significant step in the Philippines' commitment to combating human trafficking and upholding the rights and welfare of vulnerable individuals.
